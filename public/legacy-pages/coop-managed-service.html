The situation

Unifying data operations across 4,000 stores and 13 business entities

The client is one of the UK's largest retail co-operatives, operating across more than 4,000 stores and 13 distinct business entities. The complexity of managing benchmarking, reporting and data consistency across that footprint demanded a centralised approach — one that could deliver consistent insight while respecting the operational nuances of each entity.

Building and maintaining that capability in-house had become a problem the business didn't want to keep solving. The skills required to operate a modern data ecosystem at this scale span data architecture, engineering, analytics engineering, governance and visualisation — and the recruiting market for those skills had grown harder, not easier. Retaining a complete team while keeping pace with shifting business priorities was an ongoing tax on time and budget.

The brief was clear: reliable, expert data management at scale. With cost predictability. Without the in-house overhead.

"The decision wasn't about whether to outsource. It was about who they could trust to operate as part of the business, not just supply it."

The service model

A dual-tier structure, with bolt-ons that flex to need

FSP's managed service was designed around a clear, simple commercial structure — predictable enough for finance teams, flexible enough for the operational reality of a complex retail business.

Why this works

Three things that turn a managed service into a partnership

1. Clarity beats cleverness in service contracts.

The dual-tier structure was deliberately easy to read. Stakeholders across the business — finance, operations, IT — could understand exactly what was being bought, what would be delivered, and what it cost. Predictability isn't glamorous, but it's the foundation of trust in long-term operational relationships.

2. Flexibility through bolt-ons, not through contract renegotiation.

Business needs change quarterly. Procurement cycles do not. Designing the service so that capacity flexes without re-contracting was the difference between a service that adapts and one that gets in its own way.

3. Specialist access without specialist hire.

Most data teams can justify generalist roles. Specialist roles — senior data architect, analytics engineer, visualisation lead — are harder to staff and easier to underuse. The managed service unlocked instant access to those specialists, sized to actual demand rather than hopeful headcount planning.
