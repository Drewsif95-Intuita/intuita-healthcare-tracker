The strategy

Tease, deliver, reflect — calibrated to case study weight

Not every case study earns the same volume of social content. Tier 1 case studies (the strategic platforms with rich source material) carry a full three-post arc each. Tier 2 carries two posts. Tier 3 carries a single hero post. Total: 13 posts, deliberately right-sized to the audience and source material.

①

Tease (week 1 of arc)

The story — counterintuitive decision or distinctive outcome — told as narrative. Drives the click to the case study page. First-line hook is engineered for the LinkedIn feed cutoff.

②

Deliver (week 2 of arc)

A perspective piece extracting a single insight from the case study. Standalone — works for readers who didn't see post 1. Different shape (claim-based, not story-based).

③

Reflect (week 3 of arc)

List-format reflection on the broader principles. Positions the practitioner as having a philosophy, not just a project. Often outperforms the others on engagement.

★

Hero post (single)

For Tier 2 and 3 case studies. One strong post that combines hook, story and reflection. Does the work of three posts in one — used where audience scale doesn't justify a longer arc.

Mechanics

How the campaign is run, day-to-day

↻

Three rotating posters

Drew leads, with two other senior practitioners rotating through the deliver and reflect slots. No single person posts more than once every 2–3 weeks. Broadens algorithmic reach across multiple networks.

⊕

Cross-amplification

When each post goes live, the other two practitioners comment substantively within the first hour. Three minutes per post; materially better algorithm performance. Pull out a number, ask a question, add a perspective.

📅

Posting window

Tuesday or Wednesday morning UK time, 8:30–10:00am. B2B engagement peaks for London commuter audiences. Same day-of-week and time across the campaign for predictability.

🔗

Link in the first comment

Every promotional post puts the case study URL in the first comment from the author's account, not the body. LinkedIn deprioritises posts with external links in the body. 1.5–3× organic reach uplift.

📊

UTM tracking on every link

Format: ?utm_source=linkedin&utm_campaign={case}&utm_content=post{n}. Allows traffic attribution per post in GA. Five minutes of setup, weeks of attribution data.

📡

Company page reshare

24+ hours after each personal post, the FSP company page reshares with a one-line framing. Don't reshare same-day — it cannibalises the personal post's reach.

Measurement

What to track, when to adjust

The campaign has two checkpoints built in. Don't run all 14 weeks blind — use the data.

①

Per-post metrics (weekly)

Impressions, reactions, comments, reposts (LinkedIn analytics). Click-throughs and time on case study page (GA, via UTM tags). 10 minutes per week.

②

Mid-campaign review (after week 6)

Six posts in. Look for patterns: which hooks worked? Which case studies drew the most traffic? Adjust the second half of the campaign — change posting times, swap practitioner allocations, refine hooks — based on what the data says.

③

End-of-campaign retrospective (week 15)

Total inbound enquiries tagged to LinkedIn source. Total case study traffic. Best-performing posts. Worst-performing posts and why. Foundation for the next campaign cycle.
