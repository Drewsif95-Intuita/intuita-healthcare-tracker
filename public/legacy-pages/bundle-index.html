Three template approach

Why three, not one

Different case study types serve different buyer decision processes. Forcing all six into one template would either bloat the smaller stories or under-serve the bigger ones. The three templates share a single design system, but the structure and tone of each is calibrated to the audience.

Template A — Strategic Platform

For multi-quarter platform engagements. ~2,000 words. Architecture diagram, outcome stat band, platform/business products grid. Buyer: CDO, head of data, VP engineering. Reads for capability and ambition.

Template B — Analytics & Modelling

For ML, modelling and analytics engagements. ~1,400 words. Methodology diagram, capability-unlocked panel, "where else this applies" transferable IP section. Buyer: head of analytics, ops director, supply chain lead. Reads for method and rigour.

Template C — Managed Service

For ongoing service engagements. ~900 words. No architecture diagram — instead a service-tier model. Trust signal cards, partnership-toned CTA. Buyer: data leader managing budget and team. Reads for predictability and partnership.

What we need from you

Approval checklist

The fastest path through is one consolidated review meeting covering the full bundle. Specific decisions needed:

  • Confirm brand colours. The mint accent (#9FE5BD) and dark teal-navy (#0D2A2E) are sampled from fsp.co screenshots. Confirm Pantone / hex values from the brand guidelines.
  • Confirm typography. Pages currently use Inter as the FSP-equivalent sans-serif. Swap to FSP's licensed face if different.
  • Approve the placement strategy. Recommend a "Recent work" section on the Data & AI page housing 3-6 case study cards, with detail pages nested under /data-ai-4/case-studies/.
  • Approve the three templates. A (Platform), B (Analytics), C (Managed Service). Future case studies fit one of the three.
  • Approve the LinkedIn campaign plan. 14 weeks, 3 rotating posters, 13 posts. Confirm posting account ownership and company-page reshare process.
  • Identify the second and third LinkedIn posters. Two senior practitioners from the data practice to share the posting load. Names locked before campaign starts.
  • Wire UTM tracking. Confirm Google Analytics is set up to capture UTM parameters from the LinkedIn post URLs before campaign launch.
Suggested next steps

Six things to do this fortnight

Internal walkthrough

Open the six case study pages and the campaign overview. Walk through them as a coherent set. Catch any voice or framing issues before external review.

FSP marketing review meeting

Single consolidated session. Use the approval checklist on this page as the agenda. One review covers all six case studies and the campaign.

Client permissions chase

In parallel: each client sponsor approves the public version. Anonymised launch first, named upgrades follow client sign-off. Track per case study.

Lock the posting team

Confirm the second and third LinkedIn posters. Brief them on the campaign mechanics — voice, cross-amplification, posting windows.

Set up measurement

UTM templates wired into GA. Tracking dashboard for weekly review. This is the launch-blocker: don't go live without attribution.

Schedule week-by-week

Assign actual dates to each of the 14 weeks. Block the time on the calendar. Posts that don't have dates tend to slip indefinitely.
