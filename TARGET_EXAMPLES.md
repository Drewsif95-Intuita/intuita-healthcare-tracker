# Example Targets — Who to Approach and Why

Three worked examples drawn directly from the processed scorecard, chosen to show
*different* commercial rationales rather than three versions of the same story. All figures
are from the live data (TAC finance, Acute Provider Table, DMA, FDP, CQC, catchment).

A reminder on framing: a high score means a strong **commercial opportunity** (a clear
problem + the means to fund it + a reason to act), not a judgement on the hospital.

---

## 1. University Hospitals of North Midlands (RJE) — the all-rounder · Band A, rank #1

**Why it tops the list:** it is the rare trust that scores well on all three live pillars —
operational pain 68.8, budget 84.3, digital 71.8 — with nothing holding it back.

- **Clear problem:** worst on A&E 4-hour and 12-hour performance plus RTT 52-week waits — an
  urgent, board-visible urgent-and-emergency-care story. Trend is *improving*, so the
  conversation is "help us keep this going", not "rescue".
- **Can fund it:** £1.29bn operating income, a healthy **+1.6% operating margin**, in-year
  Q3 on plan (0% variance), and a £110m capital envelope. This is a trust with money and
  stability, not one in financial crisis.
- **Reason to engage now:** digital maturity is low (DMA 2.37/5) yet it is **live on the FDP
  and realising benefits** — an active data-platform programme to attach to.
- **Confidence High**, CQC Good / Well-led Good, catchment 653k.
- **Play:** UEC / A&E flow — flow analytics, discharge optimisation, A&E command centre.

## 2. University Hospitals of Derby and Burton (RTG) — the cancer-pathway play · Band A, rank #2

**Why it is different from #1:** the pain here is concentrated in **cancer and diagnostics**
(worst on Faster Diagnostic Standard, 62-day, and diagnostic >6-week waits), not UEC — a
different buyer and a different proposition.

- **Strongest budget pillar of the top tier (89.1):** £1.35bn income, the best margin of
  the leaders at **+2.4%**, £99m capital. Financially this is one of the most fundable trusts
  in England.
- **Largest digital opportunity gap among the leaders** (digital pillar 47.4, DMA 2.53) — but
  again **FDP live + benefits**, so there is a transformation route in.
- Good mortality (SHMI 0.89), CQC Good / Well-led Good, 900k catchment, *improving* trend.
- **Play:** cancer / diagnostics pathway — diagnostic capacity, pathway analytics, PTL
  management. Pair the strong finances with a specific, fundable clinical pain point.

## 3. Barts Health (R1H) — the scale / deal-size play · Band B, rank #3

**Why it earns a place despite "only" Band B:** it has by far the **largest budget signal in
England (97.7)** — £2.65bn operating income (the biggest of any acute trust), a **+3.2%
margin**, and a **£233m capital envelope**. If deal size matters, this is the trust.

- **The "why now" is regulatory as much as operational:** CQC **Requires improvement**
  overall (Well-led Good) creates board-level urgency to demonstrate improvement — a genuine
  opening, used as context, not as a stick.
- Pain is moderate (55.1) and concentrated in diagnostics and 12-hour A&E; trend improving.
- Large, **deprived** London catchment (725k, IMD decile 3.9, only 7.7% over 65) — a strong
  health-inequalities and demand-complexity narrative.
- **Play:** diagnostics / pathway plus a board-level improvement-and-assurance angle. The
  scale means even a mid-sized engagement is commercially material.

## How to read these together

| | RJE (North Midlands) | RTG (Derby & Burton) | R1H (Barts) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Band / rank | A · #1 | A · #2 | B · #3 |
| Headline rationale | balanced, no weak spot | best finances + cancer pain | biggest scale + CQC urgency |
| Pain driver | UEC / A&E | cancer / diagnostics | diagnostics / 12-hr A&E |
| Operating income | £1.29bn | £1.35bn | £2.65bn |
| Margin | +1.6% | +2.4% | +3.2% |
| CQC overall | Good | Good | Requires improvement |
| FDP | Live + benefits | Live + benefits | Live + benefits |
| Best opening play | UEC flow | cancer/diagnostics pathway | diagnostics + board assurance |

Picking across these three gives the sales team a balanced first wave: a UEC story, a cancer
story, and a large-scale account — rather than three near-identical pursuits.

## A contrast: why a higher-"opportunity" trust is *not* on the call-now list

**Nottingham University Hospitals (RX1)** has the highest raw opportunity of any trust
(`raw_opportunity_score` 82.9 — pain 84.7, budget 89.2) but is in **NOF segment 4 (intensive
support)**. The model applies the distress penalty (→ 74.9) and caps it at **Band C**, ranking
it below all non-distressed trusts (pursuit rank 99+). The signal to the sales team is correct:
genuinely attractive on paper, but **qualify the funding route before investing BD effort** —
a distressed trust may have the problem and the appetite but not the freedom to spend. This is
exactly the behaviour the distress rule is designed to produce.
